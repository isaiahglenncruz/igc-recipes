Authentic Filipino Chicken Adobo

Home

Description

First, I will introduce you to a classic Filipino dish called chicken adobo :3 It is my favorite dish and has been since I was around 15 or 16. Around that age, I learned to appreciate the sweet undertones of brown sugar under the dish's strong foreground provided by bay leaves and large amounts of garlic. This dish makes me feel very comforted, content, and sleepy.

The dish can be made with other protein sources such as pork or beef, but the use of chicken thighs or drumsticks provides the stew's broth with a thick consistency as a result of dissolved amounts of chicken skin. Consequently, my favorite substitute for chicken thighs is pork belly. Using beef as an alternative protein source is my lowest preference because of the relatively low amounts of fat in beef cuts compared to chicken thighs or pork belly.

Ingredients

  • 1 whole bulb of garlic
  • 1 cup low-sodium soy sauce
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 cup vinegar
  • 3 tbsp brown sugar
  • 6 bay leaves
  • 8 chicken thighs or drumsticks
  • Virgin olive oil to sautee, as preferred
  • 1/2 onion

Directions

  1. First, we will prepare the marinade. Start by combining the soy sauce, vinegar, water, sugar, and bay leaves. The bay leaves will be left whole.
  2. Next, take apart the bulb of garlic. Remove the skin off each clove and leave all the cloves whole. Leaving the cloves whole will allow us to retrieve individual cloves from the finished stew and use them as condiments for our rice!
  3. Add the garlic cloves to the marinade along with the chicken. Leave the skin on, so that the fat will melt off in the cooking process and thicken the soup.
  4. Let the resulting mixture marinate for 30 minutes to an hour.
  5. Next, choose your cooking pot of choice. You will want to be able to sear the 8 thighs in the pot comfortably, so make sure the pot has considerable depth and width. You will also want the pot's lid to have an open space for steam to come out of.
  6. As the desired marination time approaches, cut the onions to your desired sauteeing preference. In your desired amount of olive oil, sautee until onions are caramelized.
  7. Once the onions have cooked well, add the individual chicken and leave the marinade aside. Slightly sear each chicken appendage until you see a noticeable crisp on the outside of the chicken. My mom said this step would allow moisture to stay inside the chicken after it goes through the following steps.
  8. After the thighs are seared to your desire, add the entire marinade into the pot. Ensure that the chicken is mostly submerged in the liquid mixture. Add water if you need to.
  9. Cook at high heat for about an hour, making sure that steam is comiing out of the pot. If you were unable to find a suitable lid as described in step 5, you may also cook without a lid - just make sure that your stew isn't overflowing.
  10. After about an hour, serve the chicken and stew over rice. You can take garlic cloves from the adobo stew and spread them over your rice like butter. The liquid marinade mixture should have reduced heavily, leaving you with a glaze-like liquid sauce!
